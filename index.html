About Me

I approach research from first principles, focusing on the mechanisms that govern energy, chemical, and industrial systems. My work aims to connect fundamental understanding with practical technological development, particularly in areas where efficiency and scalability matter. I am also interested in how technology interacts with economics and public policy, and I view engineering and institutional design as complementary elements in shaping real-world outcomes. I value disciplined execution, clear strategies, and the ability to turn rigorous analysis into steady, meaningful progress.
